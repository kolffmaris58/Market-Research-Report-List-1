<p><p>The optical lenses for lithography market includes two primary types: lenses for Deep Ultraviolet (DUV) lithography and lenses for Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography. DUV lenses utilize wavelengths typically around 193 nm, crucial for traditional semiconductor manufacturing, allowing precise patterning on silicon wafers. In contrast, EUV lenses operate at shorter wavelengths, around 13.5 nm, enabling the production of smaller, more complex features in advanced chips. Both types are essential for meeting the demands of modern electronics and improving manufacturing efficiencies.</p></p>

<p><p>Optical lenses for lithography are essential components in various printing techniques used in semiconductor manufacturing. Contact printing lithography involves direct contact between the mask and substrate, offering high resolution but limited throughput. Proximity printing lithography maintains a small gap between the mask and substrate, balancing resolution and speed. Projection printing lithography utilizes multiple optical elements to project the mask image onto the substrate, enabling larger wafers and higher throughput. Other applications include specialized techniques like nanoimprint lithography and extreme ultraviolet lithography.</p></p>
